October 2025 – Workshop Highlights

In October 2025, I attended the International Conference on Computational Physics (ICCP 2025), where I presented my latest work on machine-learning-assisted simulations of complex materials. The event provided an inspiring opportunity to exchange ideas with researchers working on multi-scale modeling and data-driven approaches in physical sciences.

During my talk, I discussed how neural-network potentials can bridge the gap between quantum accuracy and large-scale efficiency. Several attendees expressed interest in potential collaborations, particularly in applying these models to heterogeneous systems.

Aside from my presentation, I participated in a roundtable discussion on open-source computational frameworks and their role in reproducible research. It was clear that open data and transparent workflows are becoming central to modern science.

Looking ahead, I plan to integrate part of this work into my upcoming graduate course on Computational Materials Science and to collaborate with a partner lab on developing a benchmark dataset for metallic glasses.
